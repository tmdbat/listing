5.0 out of 5 stars Good quality, reasonably priced and better than average sound
Reviewed in the United Kingdom on July 9, 2021
Vine Customer Review of Free Product( What's this? )
The UGREEN HiTune earphones are supplied securely protected in a plastic tray (pic 3) inside a good looking and recyclable box (pics 1-2). They are very well made for the price and I like that the individual buds are colour coded as well as letter marked to distinguish the left from the right side. The set includes the headphones, some alternative sizes of the silicon inserts and some instructions (pic 3-4). There is an in-line microphone and volume control unit below the right earbud (pic 4). This is a bit of a compromise, I am guessing because while it is not a bad position for the microphone, I found the volume control awkward to use. I would have preferred the latter a little lower down the lead.

I tried it with my faithful old iPad2, which is definitely showing its age but is still the best sounding device that I have. I ran a few CD rips through iTunes and was soon enjoying the sound. I have owned and tested a fair few sets of headphones across the range of prices. I must say I was very pleasantly surprised by the spaciousness and frequency range of these UGREENs. On a sound per pound basis they seem to me to make a lot of sense. They work well with voice and music and were useful for making calls on my suitably equipped phone (that is it still has a 3.5mm audio socket fitted).

Musically these headphones do a good job of reproducing the depth and impact of bass notes. Running through my usual test tracks I was impressed by the thunder at the beginning of Brothers in Arms by Dire Straits and the very low notes from a double bass on a couple of Bowles Brothers Tracks. At the opposite end the Synthesiser on Camel's Never let go is clear and goes very high indeed. Similarly, the clarity of high notes on Paul Simon's Graceland album is impressive. Mid-range is also well catered for and there is an overall impression of airiness and space. Switching to classical music the Clarinet on Mozart's Clarinet Concerto is full-bodied and rich. Detail is also up to scratch and I can hear the click of the Clarinet keys as the performer makes the different notes. In short these are pretty good headphones and for the price they are a bit of a bargain. Put it this way, I have often heard worse and those were often more expensive. As a cheaper alternative to say, Sennheiser, Sony or SoundMagic these make a strong case for themselves.

On the downside they do sound better at moderate to high volumes and lose a little sparkle at lower levels. They also sound better with good quality rips again losing a little of their sparkle with lower resolution MP3 files. I prefer lossless or .WAV files and so they worked well for me. Incidentally, they work very well with the HD and Ultra HD content that is now available to Amazon Music Unlimited subscribers.

For £11 at the time of writing there is very little not to like about these headphones. The are well made and sound and look the part. They work equally well with music and audio and do a good job when answering calls. What more do you need. Well a case would be nice but one could easily be sourced for a few pounds from the Amazon website, among others. I happen to have a spare one so all is good. These will be my travel companions this summer.

UGREEN HiTune 3.5mm Earphones, In-Ear Wired Headphones Earphones with Mic High Definition Stereo Audio In-Line Control Noise Isolation Earbuds Wired

In the box.
The wired earbuds with 3.5mm jack plug. They have an inline volume control.

Quality.
Considering the asking price they are well made and feel quite tough. The lead is long and can easily be threaded through rucksacks etc.

In use.
I used them with a Samsung phone and found the sound to be more than acceptable. They are definitely a lot better than the ones supplied with many phones. I have some very expensive earbuds here and these did not let themselves down sonically. The bass is not that deep but what there is has some definition. There is also a built-in mic for call taking. This does work but it picks up all the background noise as well so you need to hold it close.

Overall.
These are a budget option with above average sound. If you just want a cheap replacement pair then these will do fine. They will be great for out doing sports or commuting. At this price I can’t really fault them. Yes, you can get better sound but you need to spend more.
